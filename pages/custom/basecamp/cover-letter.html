HEY Basecamp,


I'm King'ori Maina. About 7 years ago, I stumbled on the signalvnoise.com blog back when you were still called 37signals. I've been an avid subscriber since then. Basecamp's ideas have shaped my views on a lot of topics; starting from work culture, remote work, product design, business focus, privacy etc.

If you would have told me then, that I would be applying for a System Administrators role on your Operations Team, I wouldn't have believed you. But as fate would have it, here were are. Am I scared? Heck Yeah! Despite that, I'm up for the challenge because I believe we would be a good fit for each other from a technical, cultural and values point of view.

Why You?

YOU HAVE GREAT PRODUCT DESIGN
You hire smart people who build awesome products. Basecamp and HEY are both fantastic products with thoughtful and simple designs. I loved reading through how HEY was Shaped Up.

YOU HAVE LASER FOCUS
As a company, Basecamp has constantly been able to do more by doing less. Less hours, less products & less tools. You're clear about what you don't want and you're not afraid to say no even when it's unpopular.

YOU HAVE COURAGE
Fighting for what you believe in and standing up to bullies or shakedowns is something you do. Even if it's a trillion dollar company like Apple. You fight for what's right even if it's hard and dangerous.

YOU SWEAT THE DETAILS
You care about the design itself as well as the design process. Always starting from the epicenter and working outwards. You care about user interactions and even keyboard shortcuts. You care about speed. Heck, you even care about the size of JavaScript on the page! No detail is too small.

YOU VALUE OPEN-SOURCE
Ruby on Rails finds its roots at Basecamp. So does Turbolinks, Trix, Stimulus, Local Time & Geared Pagination. You always share the best parts with the community. I'm looking forward to the open-sourcing of the new techniques employed by HEY to take advantage of HTTP/2 changes.

Why Me?

REMOTE FIT
I'm a Kenyan and live between Nairobi, Kenya and Cape Town, South Africa. I’m based in the Cape Town office of a London company that has more than 30 nationalities. I work in a multi-timezone, multi-cultural & remote environment. In fact, I've been managing my team while remote for the past 2½ years! Remote is in my DNA.

GOALS FIT
I've helped build an in-house Heroku-like system that supports about 60 developers to deploy applications, cut releases, backup/restore environments & manage configuration. I believe the main objective for an Operations Team is to make deployments a non-event and to make infrastructure stable and boring. Shipping should not be scary.

VALUES FIT
Being straightforward, fair, levelheaded, generous and showing independence of thought are values I ascribe to. Underlying all these values is empathy, which is one of my strongest qualities. With empathy one is always working out the best for their colleague or customer. Always considerate of the other person from their point of view.

TECHNICAL FIT
Starting off a software developer, Ruby is my favourite programming language and Ruby on Rails is my go-to framework. I've been running Rails in production for most of my professional career, including my side-projects. Ruby on Rails is my bread and butter.

Right now, as a DevOps engineer, I've successfully designed and managed scalable and stable infrastructure running on self-managed Kubernetes clusters on AWS for the past 4 years (starting from v1.4.7). I also have production experience with a lot of the tools used by your Operations Team i.e. Docker, GitHub Actions, MySQL, Redis, Elasticsearch, Terraform and Percona Tools. I probably don't know everything about your systems but I'd be familiar with your standard stack, right off the bat.

I'm also familiar with Linux and its fundamentals, good at shell scripting and able to build microservices and CLIs in Go. I have all the hallmarks of a competent system administrator.

While I enjoy working with computers, I do have other interests. I love spending time with my wife (Gugu), dining out, going on long roadtrips and taking photos while travelling. When I get the chance, I play the bass and geek out on bass guitar theory and history.

Working at Basecamp would be a huge opportunity for me to grow. I'd love to have the chance to chat more about my work and who I am as a person. So, I hope we can continue the conversation.



King'ori Maina — kingori.co.
